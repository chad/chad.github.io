Charity and Testing at the Hoedown

2007.08.01

Now that my domain is alive again (sorry if you tried to send me email within the last week and a half), I wante to quickly mention that Bruce Tate, Marcel Molina and I will be doing a charity workshop on Ruby testing techniques at The Ruby Hoedown in Raleigh, NC. As was started by the Dave and Mike with The Rails Guidebook, we’ll be suggesting a small donation ($50) to charity (this time the Food Bank of Central and Eastern North Carolina). We’ve been quiet for a little longer than we should have on this, so please do pop over and register if you’re interested in testing in Ruby, helping the hungry, or both.

Note that you don’t have to attend the session to donate. So if you’re totally uninterested in testing (shame on you!), a Ruby skeptic, don’t even know what programming means or don’t want to travel to Raleigh, feel free to add some dollars to the cause.
